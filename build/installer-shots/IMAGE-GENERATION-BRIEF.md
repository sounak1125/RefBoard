# RefBoard Premium Installer — Image Generation Brief

## Objective

Create five cohesive cinematic feature shots for the RefBoard Windows installer.

The sequence should feel like a premium creative-software launch film: original, elegant, restrained, and unmistakably connected to RefBoard. It must communicate real RefBoard features without becoming a literal screenshot collage or imitating NVIDIA, Adobe, or another company's installer.

The five images must look like consecutive moments from one continuous visual world.

## Product context

RefBoard is a private, local-first visual reference, moodboard, annotation, and animatics application for Windows.

Core capabilities represented in this sequence:

- Infinite moodboard canvas for images, notes, and visual organization.
- Paste and drag references from the clipboard, browser, or local files.
- Original-quality images with crop, rotate, flip, grayscale, zoom, grouping, and alignment.
- Drawing, annotations, notes, arrows, and visual idea connections.
- Animatics with layered visuals, text, audio, waveforms, timing, and track controls.
- Board saving, full-resolution export, original-file export, Premiere export, and After Effects export.

## Shared visual system

### Creative direction

Premium cinematic 3D motion-design keyframes for a flagship creative application. The images should feel physical, dimensional, and art-directed rather than like generic technology wallpapers.

Use these recurring RefBoard motifs:

- A precise field of tiny cool-gray canvas dots receding into depth.
- Floating borderless reference-image planes.
- Smoked glass and translucent creative layers.
- Satin anodized-black structural material.
- Thin cobalt selection geometry and alignment guides.
- Restrained hand-drawn strokes and connection paths.
- Timeline ribbons that evolve naturally from image planes.
- Sparse warm-amber accents for contrast.

### Palette

- Near black: `#080A0F`
- Charcoal: `#10141C`
- Deep navy: `#111B2A`
- Slate: `#1C293B`
- RefBoard blue: `#168BFF`
- Highlight blue: `#65C7FF`
- Soft white: `#EAF2FF`
- Sparse amber accent: `#F2A34A`

Blue is the primary energy and interaction color. Amber should occupy less than five percent of any frame.

### Materials and lighting

- Physically plausible smoked glass.
- Subtle soft reflections and realistic depth separation.
- Cool silver edge highlights.
- Controlled cobalt emission, never broad neon glow.
- Very light volumetric atmosphere.
- Fine filmic contrast with deep but readable shadows.
- Crisp antialiasing and clean small-scale silhouettes.

### Continuity

All five shots should share:

- The same black/navy environment.
- The same dot-grid scale and perspective.
- The same glass thickness and edge treatment.
- The same cobalt and amber light behavior.
- A consistent camera language.
- A progressive narrative from gathering ideas to exporting finished work.

The sequence should feel like one camera moving through a single RefBoard universe:

`Collect → Refine → Think → Animate → Deliver`

## Typography and exact copy

If the generation model is reliable at text, render the following copy exactly. Do not paraphrase, change capitalization, or invent additional text.

If the model cannot guarantee perfect text, generate the cinematic background with a completely clean left text-safe area and add the typography afterward in a deterministic design tool.

Typography direction:

- Typeface: Inter or a visually equivalent modern grotesk.
- Eyebrow: Inter Medium, uppercase, wide tracking, cool blue-gray.
- Heading: Inter SemiBold, sentence case, soft white.
- Description: Inter Regular, cool light gray, comfortable line height.
- Feature chips: small dark-glass capsules with a one-pixel cool-gray border.
- No gradients inside text.
- No oversized marketing typography covering the artwork.
- Maintain the same baseline grid across all five frames.

Recommended layout:

- RefBoard mark or small `REFBOARD` wordmark at `x: 140`, `y: 112`.
- Eyebrow at `x: 140`, `y: 420`.
- Heading begins near `x: 140`, `y: 480`.
- Description begins 44–56 pixels below the heading.
- Feature chips begin 52–64 pixels below the description.
- Small sequence indicator `01 — 05` may appear near the bottom-left.
- Do not draw installer navigation buttons into the image; those belong to the installer UI layer.

## Quality-control checklist

Reject and regenerate a shot if any of the following are true:

- It looks like a processed screenshot instead of original cinematic art.
- The represented feature is not understandable at a glance.
- The shot does not share the same world, materials, or lighting as the other four.
- The left typography zone contains important artwork or bright highlights.
- The image becomes unreadable when reduced to approximately 900 × 506 pixels.
- Text is misspelled, malformed, low-resolution, or inconsistent with the exact copy.
- The RefBoard mark is geometrically altered or replaced.
- The visual language resembles NVIDIA, Adobe, gaming software, or generic cyberpunk art.
- Blue emission overwhelms material definition.
- Amber becomes a dominant color.
